Visit the Heart of India

Madhya Pradesh

Madhya Pradesh has a rich history and is home to many ancient monuments and temples. Madhya Pradesh is also known for its rich wildlife and biodiversity. It is home to several national parks and wildlife sanctuaries.

Unique Attractions

BandhavGarh National Park

Formerly a hunting ground for the Maharajas of Rewa, the Bandhavgarh National Park is world-renowned as a tiger reserve and it is known to have the highest density of Bengal tigers in the world.

Read more

Sanchi Stupa

Sanchi Stupa is a significant Buddhist monument located in the town of Sanchi in Madhya Pradesh, India. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered to be one of the oldest stone structures in India. The stupa was built by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE to honor the relics of Buddha.

Read more

Khajuraho Temple

The Khajuraho Temples are a group of Hindu and Jain temples located in Khajuraho, a town in the Chhatarpur district of Madhya Pradesh, India. The temples were built by the Chandela dynasty between the 10th and 12th centuries and are known for their intricate carvings and sculptures depicting various aspects of Indian culture, mythology, and religion.

Read more

Gwalior Fort

Gwalior Fort is a historic fort located in the city of Gwalior. The fort has a rich history and is considered to be one of the most important monuments in India. It was built in the 8th century by the Rajput king Suraj Sen and has since been ruled by several dynasties, including the Mughals, Marathas, and Scindias.

Read more

Dhuandhar Falls

Dhuandhar Falls is a magnificent waterfall that cascades down a rocky gorge, creating a misty spray that gives the falls their name (Dhuandhar translates to "smoke cascade"). The falls are particularly impressive during the monsoon season when the water flow is at its highest.

Explore Heart of India

Nature

Madhya Pradesh is known for its diverse and rich natural beauty. The state is home to several national parks, wildlife sanctuaries, forests, and rivers that attract nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts from all over the world.

Heritage

The state is home to several ancient monuments, temples, forts, and palaces that reflect the diverse and fascinating history of the region.some examples of the heritage sites in Madhya Pradesh are sanchi stupa, Gwalior Fort, mandu etc

Adavnture

Madhya Pradesh is home to several beautiful trekking trails that take you through dense forests, hills, and valleys. The Pachmarhi Biosphere Reserve is a popular destination for trekking, offering several trails of varying difficulty levels.

Sprituality

Madhya Pradesh is a state in central India that is known for its rich cultural heritage and spiritual significance. The state is home to several important religious sites, ashrams, and pilgrimage centers that attract visitors from all over the world.

Wildlife

The state is home to several national parks, wildlife sanctuaries, and reserves that offer visitors a chance to experience the beauty and diversity of the natural world.some examples of the wildlife you can find in Madhya Pradesh Tigers, Leopards, Sloth Bears etc

Food

Madhya Pradesh offers a rich culinary experience to visitors. The cuisine of Madhya Pradesh is influenced by the local culture and geography, and is known for its rich and spicy flavors. some of which are Dal Bafla, Bhutte ki Kees, Poha and etc

Destination

Indore

It is the largest city in the state and is known for its rich history. The city is known for its textile and manufacturing industries, as well as its role as a hub for trade and commerce in central India

Ujjain

Ujjain is considered one of the seven holy cities in Hinduism, and is an important pilgrimage site for Hindus. The city is home to several ancient temples, including the Mahakaleshwar Temple

Mandu

Mandu is known for its impressive architecture, which includes several palaces, mosques, and tombs. Some of the popular architectural landmarks include Jahaz Mahal,Hindola Mahal etc.

Orchha

Orchha is known for its impressive architecture, which includes several palaces, temples, and forts. Some of which are Jahangir Mahal, Raj Mahal, Chaturbhuj Temple, and Laxmi Narayan Temple

Gwalior

Gwalior is known for its rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its art, music, and cuisine. The city is famous for its traditional Gwalior cuisine, which includes dishes like kachori, samosa etc.

Bhopal

Bhopal is famous for its two man-made lakes, the Upper Lake and the Lower Lake. These lakes are a popular spot for boating and other water activities, and are surrounded by parks and gardens.
